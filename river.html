Travel lag

Headwater rain takes ~2-5 days to reach Toronto (leading edge ~1-2 days when flows are already high; full peak up to ~a week for big, widespread events). The discharge chart already routes this for you.

Watch discharge, not height. The New Cumberland & Pike Island lock-and-dams hold the river’s height nearly constant, so stage is useless. They don’t mask discharge (flow), which is what tracks high, fast, muddy water.

Clarity is inferred, then calibrated. No real-time turbidity sensor exists near Toronto, so the verdict blends flow LEVEL (vs the season’s normal) with DIRECTION (mud rides the rising limb; water clears on the way down). Log trips with --log 1..5 to tune it to your eyes.

Rain is shown, not double-counted. The GloFAS discharge forecast already bakes in forecast rainfall + soil moisture + routing. The rain tab is there so you can see the pulse coming and understand the lag — it isn’t added into the score again.
